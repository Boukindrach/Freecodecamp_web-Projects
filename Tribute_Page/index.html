Steve Jobs

The Man Who Revolutionized Technology

Steve Jobs, co-founder of Apple Inc., transformed multiple industries and how we interact with technology. From a garage startup to becoming one of the most influential innovators of the 20th century, Jobs reshaped personal computing, mobile communication, digital animation, and consumer electronics.

Here's a time line of Steve Jobs' life:

  • 1955 - Born on February 24 in San Francisco, California, and immediately adopted by Paul and Clara Jobs.
  • 1972 - Graduates from Homestead High School in Cupertino, California.
  • 1973 - Briefly attends Reed College in Portland, Oregon, but drops out after one semester.
  • 1974 - Works at Atari as a technician, known for his creative problem-solving skills.
  • 1976 - Co-founds Apple Computer with Steve Wozniak in the Jobs family garage, creating the Apple I computer.
  • 1977 - Introduces the Apple II, the first successful mass-produced microcomputer with color graphics.
  • 1980 - Apple goes public, creating more millionaires in a single day than any company in history.
  • 1984 - Launches the Macintosh, the first personal computer with a graphical user interface, introduced by the groundbreaking "1984" Super Bowl commercial.
